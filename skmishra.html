Research

Expertise: Climate Analysis, Modeling, and Solution

Professor Mishra's research focuses on climate modeling, analysis, and developing solutions for climate-related challenges. His work spans various aspects of atmospheric sciences with a particular emphasis on the Indian monsoon system and global climate change.

He leads the DST Centre of Excellence for Climate Information at IIT Delhi, which aims to improve climate projections and develop actionable climate information for policymakers and stakeholders.

Course Description

The Earth's Atmosphere: Physical Principles (ASL360). (3-0-0-3): This course is on the physical principles of the Earth's atmosphere. The course will begin with the forces and energy governing the atmospheric flows and regulating the weather and climate of the planet. Physical processes such as atmospheric convection will be discussed in great detail. How the atmospheric flows transport mass, momentum, and energy in space will be described. The general features of the today's atmosphere will be explained. Emphases will be given on the need and art of atmospheric modeling and future projection of the atmosphere for the next century. The course is very conceptual in nature with a strong research flavour, and will introduce undergraduate students to the art of research in context of the subject.

DST Centre of Excellence for Climate Information

About the Center

The DST Centre of Excellence for Climate Information at IIT Delhi, led by Professor Saroj Kanta Mishra, is dedicated to advancing climate science and providing actionable climate information for decision-making.

The center focuses on improving climate models, developing regional climate projections, and translating scientific knowledge into practical solutions for climate adaptation and mitigation.

Key Objectives

  • • Develop an India-centric climate model with improved parameterizations
  • • Generate high-resolution climate projections for India and surrounding regions
  • • Develop machine learning approaches for climate model improvement
  • • Provide actionable climate information for policymakers and stakeholders

Current Projects

  • DST Centre of Excellence for Climate Information (Phase II)

    2024-2029

    This phase focuses on developing decision-relevant climate information products and services for various sectors including agriculture, water resources, and disaster management.

  • DST Centre of Excellence in Climate Modeling

    2017-2024

    This project aims to improve climate model physics with focus on the Indian monsoon system and develop an India-centric climate model.
